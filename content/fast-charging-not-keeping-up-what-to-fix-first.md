## Fast diagnosis in 5 minutes (no tools)

Run this quick check:
- **A) Charging time window:** Do you reliably get enough time to refill?
- **B) Charging power:** Is your real-world input strong and consistent enough to recover?
- **C) Storage headroom:** Is your battery size leaving too little margin after normal nights?
- **D) Load discipline:** Are spikes and routines undoing your refill gains?

## The 4 failure modes (what’s actually happening)

### Mode 1: Window too small (you cannot charge long enough)

**Symptoms**
- You only get short or inconsistent charging sessions.
- You charge often but remain behind by the end of the week.

**Common causes**
- Rules, parking, or schedule limits reduce usable charging windows.
- Quiet-hours constraints break your recovery rhythm.

**Smallest next move**
- Lock one repeatable weekly refill routine first.
- Use **[Shore Power Refill Window Plan](/shore-power-refill-window-plan/)** and **[Drive Recovery Window Plan](/drive-recovery-window-plan/)**.
- If timing rules are your bottleneck, route to **[Best Fast-Charging Power Stations for Quiet Hours](/best-fast-charging-power-stations-for-quiet-hours/)**.

### Mode 2: Input bottleneck (your AC/DC input is low or inconsistent)

**Symptoms**
- Claimed fast charging is not reflected in your real sessions.
- Refill varies heavily from one day to the next.

**Common causes**
- Inconsistent access quality and interrupted sessions.
- Charging behavior that looks fast on paper but is not repeatable in your routine.

**Smallest next move**
- Plan from repeatable real-world refill, not best-case numbers.
- Label assumptions as **Spec / Reported / Estimate** and avoid over-trusting one spec line.
- Route via **[Buyer Checklist](/fast-charging-power-station-buyer-checklist/)** before any purchase.

### Mode 3: Capacity mismatch (storage too small for nightly load)

**Symptoms**
- You recover during the day but wake up with low margin.
- One heavier night collapses the next day.

**Common causes**
- Baseline overnight demand is higher than expected.
- Too little buffer to absorb bad-weather or busy-week variance.

**Smallest next move**
- Keep the **700Wh+ minimum** floor for recovery-first planning.
- Reduce baseline drain first, then reassess whether capacity is truly the limiter.
- If upgrade is still needed, route to **[Best Fast-Charging Power Stations Under $1000](/best-fast-charging-power-stations-under-1000/)**.

### Mode 4: Load chaos (spikes, routines, heat, fans, fridge cycles)

**Symptoms**
- Some days are stable, then one spiky day wipes out progress.
- You are fine on easy weeks and unstable on bad weeks.

**Common causes**
- Loads are not batched around refill windows.
- Climate and schedule swings create unpredictable demand.

**Smallest next move**
- Switch to a temporary stable-mode routine for 3-7 days.
- Use **[Van battery drift diagnosis](/van-battery-drift-diagnosis/)** to identify which loads drive drift.
- Execute the smallest behavior fix from **[Recovery Moves Index](/recovery-moves-index/)**.

## Fix-first order (do these before upgrading)

Routines beat bigger batteries in most failure cases.

Start here:
1) Set one non-negotiable refill window for this week.
2) Pair one backup window (shore or drive) so one miss does not break recovery.
3) Batch heavy-use tasks into refill windows when possible.
4) Pre-cool or pre-heat during charging windows instead of after.
5) Reduce always-on load during drift weeks.
6) Use a quiet-hours plan if rules constrain your timing.
7) Track 3-5 days of start/end battery trend before any buying decision.
8) Keep your plan simple enough to repeat on stressful weeks.
9) Only after routine is stable, reassess whether faster recharge or more capacity is needed.
