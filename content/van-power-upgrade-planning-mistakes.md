## Mistake 1 — Upgrading mid-stress (panic timing)

Pattern:
- Buying while everything feels urgent.

Why it backfires:
- Stress hides root causes and pushes short-term decisions.

Do this instead:
- Run a bad-week protocol, run the checklist, and decide after you see a normal-week signal.

Use:
- **[Tier 2 Bad Week Protocol](/tier-2-bad-week-protocol/)**
- **[Van Power Weekly Reset Routine](/van-power-weekly-reset-routine/)**

## Mistake 2 — Shopping for a price point instead of a recovery plan

Pattern:
- Picking a tier first, then trying to force your routine into it.

Why it backfires:
- Price-only decisions ignore whether your refill plan is actually executable.

Do this instead:
- Use price-drift-safe thinking: under now vs watch list, then match your routine.

Use:
- **[Portable Power Stations Under $500](/best-of/power-stations-under-500/)**
- **[Best Fast-Charging Power Stations Under $1000](/best-fast-charging-power-stations-under-1000/)**

## Mistake 3 — Assuming your recharge method will be available every day

Pattern:
- Planning as if your preferred refill path always works.

Why it backfires:
- Quiet hours, parking constraints, clouds, and work blocks can remove your expected window.

Do this instead:
- Plan layered recovery around real-day constraints, not ideal days.

- **[Quiet Hours Recovery Plan](/quiet-hours-recovery-plan/)**

## Mistake 4 — Not testing the routine before buying more gear

Pattern:
- Buying first, then discovering workflow leaks.

Why it backfires:
- You may pay to mask routine issues instead of fixing them.

Do this instead:
- Run daily checks for a week, find leaks, then decide.

## Mistake 5 — Ignoring “friction costs” (space, noise, weight, heat, setup time)

Pattern:
- Choosing gear only on specs and pricing.

Why it backfires:
- Friction shows up daily and erodes routine compliance.

Do this instead:
- Plan around lived tradeoffs so the system stays usable under stress.

- **[Workday Power Boundaries](/workday-power-boundaries/)**

## Mistake 6 — No shared agreement (couples) before upgrades

Pattern:
- Upgrading gear without aligning on baseline priorities.

Why it backfires:
- New gear can still create the same old conflict loop.

Do this instead:
- Set baseline and comfort agreements before you buy.
