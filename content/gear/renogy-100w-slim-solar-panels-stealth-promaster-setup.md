## Real-World Performance

### Daily Harvest Reality

Nominal wattage is marketing-friendly, but real harvest depends on:

- sun angle,
- shade,
- panel cleanliness,
- temperature,
- controller quality,
- parking orientation.

In practical use, a 300W slim setup can cover a lot for efficient van lifers: fridge cycling, lights, router, and device charging. It struggles only when users add heavy all-electric cooking without backup charging.

### Stealth Benefits in Urban and Mixed Travel

A lower-profile array is easier to live with in mixed environments. I found it less attention-grabbing than bulkier roof builds, especially when paired with careful cable routing and clean mounting lines.

### Reliability and Maintenance

Solar reliability is mostly install quality plus maintenance discipline:

- inspect sealant on schedule,
- check bracket torque,
- clean panels regularly,
- watch cable rub points.

Do that consistently and panel systems can stay boring in the best possible way.

## Installation Notes from a 2017 ProMaster 2500 Build

My 3-panel stealth setup worked because I treated mounting and wiring as equal priorities. Panel quality means nothing if your roof work is sloppy.

What mattered most:

- rigid bracket strategy with proper seal stack,
- cable paths that avoid pooling water zones,
- protected roof entry gland,
- conservative strain relief and service loops.

I also planned around future access. If one component fails later, you should not need to destroy half your roof install.

## Common Buyer Mistakes

- Chasing max wattage without roof-fit planning.
- Ignoring shade impact from roof accessories.
- Using low-quality connectors or rushed crimps.
- Assuming all "100W" panels behave the same in heat and partial shade.

## FAQ

### Should I wire series or parallel?

Depends on controller, shade profile, and voltage strategy. There is no single always-right answer.

### Is Renogy still a good pick vs BougeRV?

## Buyer Checklist Before You Purchase

Before buying roof solar, answer these questions clearly:

- What is your average daily watt-hour demand?
- How often do you park in partial shade?
- Do you have alternator or shore backup for weak-sun periods?
- Are you prioritizing stealth profile or max roof wattage?
- Do you have a realistic maintenance routine for cleaning and inspections?

A good **stealth solar van setup** starts with load honesty. If your daily demand is high, plan for supplemental input instead of hoping nominal panel wattage saves you.
