When the battery is low, **everything feels personal**. Heat, devices, hunger, bad sleep, noise—your nervous system treats it like an emergency.

This page gives you a short **reset script** you can use *in the moment* to stop the spiral and switch into **Recovery mode thinking**.

If you haven’t set shared rules yet, start here: **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**.  
If you want a repeatable structure, keep this open: **[Shared Van Power Check-In Template](/shared-van-power-check-in-template/)**.

---

## What this script does (and what it doesn’t)

**This script is not** about solving the whole trip, picking a new power station, or “winning” the argument.

**It is** a fast pivot from blame → **plan**, using the same framework as the **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**:
- **Normal** when you can coast  
- **Conservation** when you need to slow burn  
- **Recovery** when you must restore margin

When battery is low and stress is high, assume you’re in **Recovery** until proven otherwise.

---

## The 30–60 second reset script (say it out loud)

Use the exact words below or adapt them to your voice. The point is **tone and sequence**, not perfection.

### Version A: Simple and direct
1. **Name the moment**
> “We’re both stressed, and the battery is low. This is a power problem, not a relationship problem.”

2. **Agree on the goal**
> “Our goal is to get stable again—not to argue about how we got here.”

3. **Choose the mode**
> “Let’s switch to **Recovery mode** for now.”

4. **Pick one next action**
> “Next step: we do the **quick check**, then decide *one* recovery move.”

5. **Set a short timeout**
> “Can we pause the debate until after the check-in?”

**Then do the check:**  
- Morning framework: **[Morning Van Power Check](/morning-van-power-check/)**  
- Night framework: **[Overnight Van Power Checklist](/overnight-van-power-checklist/)**

---

### Version B: Softer / more emotional safety
> “I’m feeling the pressure and I think you are too. I don’t want power stress to turn into a fight.  
> Can we take one minute, switch to **Recovery mode**, and do the quick check together?”

Then use the **[Shared Van Power Check-In Template](/shared-van-power-check-in-template/)**.

---

### Version C: One-person starter (when only one of you is regulated)
> “I’m getting reactive. I’m going to reset us: **Recovery mode**, quick check, one next step. I’m on your team.”

Then initiate the check and offer two choices:
- “Do you want to run the checklist, or should I?”

## The “don’t make it worse” rule (during Recovery mode)

When you’re in Recovery mode, your job is **to stop leaks** first.

Use these guardrails:
- **No blame audits** (“who caused this”) until stability returns.
- **No gear debates** in the heat of the moment.
- **No multitasking** through the check.
- **No new loads** unless both people explicitly agree.

If you keep repeating the same fight, read this next: **[Van Power Planning Mistakes](/van-power-planning-mistakes/)**.
