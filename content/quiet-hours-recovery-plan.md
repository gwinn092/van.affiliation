Quiet hours change the game.

Sometimes you *can’t* run obvious recovery options. Maybe you’re in a campground with strict noise rules, stealth parking, a crowded area, or you just don’t have access to shore power.

This page gives you a recovery-first way to stay stable when you’re constrained:
- protect baseline
- stop leaks early
- pick recovery moves that don’t rely on being loud or obvious
- avoid the “late-night panic” spiral

If you want the full ladder this plugs into, start here: **[Van Power Recovery Stack](/van-power-recovery-stack/)**.

---

## The quiet-hours rule: prevent drift before the night starts

Quiet hours punish late decisions.

If you wait until you’re already behind at night, your options shrink fast. The win is switching earlier:
- earlier mode shifts
- earlier leak-stopping
- earlier recovery planning

Use:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**  
- **[Overnight Van Power Checklist](/overnight-van-power-checklist/)**

## Step 1: Choose your mode *before* quiet hours

If the night has constraints, don’t pretend it’s a Normal night.

Default logic:
- **Normal** only if you’re already stable and recovery is easy tomorrow
- **Conservation** if you need to protect baseline through the night
- **Recovery** if you’re behind or conditions are harsh

Use:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

## Step 2: Stop the leaks that ruin stealth nights

Quiet hours often fail because of “small” habits that run for hours.

Two big leak sources:
- leaving AC pathways available when you’re not actively using them
- AC-first workflows when DC would work

Use:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**  
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

Quiet-hours rule:
> If it doesn’t protect baseline, it doesn’t get to run all night.

## Step 3: Define your night baseline (and agree on it)

Quiet hours require clarity. Baseline is what you protect without debate.

Write baseline as categories:
- sleep protection needs (your reality + season)
- minimum lighting for safety
- minimum communication/logistics stability

If you share the rig, define baseline together:

## Step 4: Pick tomorrow’s recovery move before you sleep

Quiet hours isn’t the time to “hope.”

Choose one recovery plan for tomorrow:
- shore power routine (when available)
- driving recovery (when routine supports it)
- solar behavior aligned with conditions

## Step 5: Use the “one change” rule at night

When you’re constrained, don’t stack changes. That creates chaos and conflict.

At night:
- stop one leak **or**
- pause one category **or**
- tighten baseline

Then reassess in the morning.
