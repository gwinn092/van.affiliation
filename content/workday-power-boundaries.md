Remote work in a van turns power into a **promise**.

On a workday, “we’ll figure it out” isn’t a plan. If you can’t keep your devices stable, everything else gets stressful—fast.

This page gives you a recovery-first way to run workdays:
- define a **work baseline**
- stop workflow leaks
- set boundaries that don’t require constant negotiation
- keep your system recoverable even when comfort pressure rises

If you want the full ladder this plugs into, start here: **[Van Power Recovery Stack](/van-power-recovery-stack/)**.

---

## The workday rule: baseline first, comfort second

On workdays, you’re not trying to maximize comfort. You’re trying to maximize **stability**.

Workday stability comes from two decisions made early:
1) what counts as **work baseline**
2) what gets paused first if you drift into Conservation or Recovery

Mode framework:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

If you keep sliding into late-day panic, add the weekly prevention layer:
- **[Van Power Weekly Reset Routine](/van-power-weekly-reset-routine/)**

### CAVL Field Notes (real-world experience)
- On humid workdays, comfort pressure can quietly extend runtimes and push drift into the evening.
- Heat plus weak recovery windows is where remote-work stability usually breaks first.
- Picking the refill move early on workdays tends to reduce both stress and conflict by late afternoon.

## Step 1: Define your Work Baseline (protected category)

Work baseline is what you need to do the job without scrambling.

Write baseline as categories, not devices:
- essential computing + essential connectivity
- minimum lighting for focused work
- the simplest comfort needed to stay functional (climate matters)

Heat note: baseline expands in heat because sleep and focus collapse without it.
- **[Heat-Wave Van Power Recovery](/heat-wave-van-power-recovery/)**

If you share the rig, this needs to be agreed on explicitly:
- **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)**

## Step 2: Pick your workday mode *in the morning*
Workdays fail when you postpone mode choice and try to “earn” it later.

Morning anchor:
- **[Morning Van Power Check](/morning-van-power-check/)**

A simple default:
- **Normal** if recovery is easy and consistent today
- **Conservation** if you need to protect work baseline and prevent drift
- **Recovery** if you’re already behind or conditions are harsh

Mode system:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

## Step 3: Stop workflow leaks (this is where most workdays are won)

Workdays often leak power through habits, not necessities.

Two common leak sources:
- **inverter habits** (AC availability all day “just in case”)
- **AC-first workflows** when a DC option exists

Use these guides as your leak-stop checklist:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

Workday rule that prevents drift:
> Choose one “always-on” habit to stop for the day.

## Step 4: Set three workday boundaries (simple, enforceable)

Boundaries only work if they’re clear enough to follow when tired.

### Boundary A: “Work baseline stays”
If anything gets cut later, it is not the work baseline.

### Boundary B: “Optional comfort is conditional”
Comfort is not banned. It’s conditional on staying recoverable.
Use:
- **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)**

### Boundary C: “If we argue, we’re overdue for a reset”
If stress is rising, don’t negotiate while reactive.
Use:
- **[Van Power Conflict Reset Script](/van-power-conflict-reset-script/)**

If you’re already in Recovery mode, use the pause-first categories:
- **[Recovery Mode Load Priorities](/recovery-mode-load-priorities/)**

## Step 5: Choose one recovery plan for the workday
Workdays are not the time to “hope” solar cooperates.

Choose one primary recovery plan you can execute:
- shore power routine (when available)
- driving recovery (when your day includes movement)
- solar behavior aligned with parking and conditions

Use these frameworks:
- **[Shore Power Recovery Routine](/shore-power-recovery-routine/)**  
- **[Solar vs Alternator Charging](/solar-vs-alternator-charging/)**  
- **[Bluetti Fast Charging](/bluetti-fast-charging/)**

- **[Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan](/cloudy-week-power-recovery-plan/)**

## Step 6: End the workday clean (so the night doesn’t spiral)
Workdays often “borrow” from the night. That’s how you end up depleted and arguing.

Before dinner or before you settle in, do the short reset:
- “What mode are we in now?”
- “Are we stable for the night?”
- “What’s tomorrow’s first recovery move?”

Night anchor:
- **[Overnight Van Power Checklist](/overnight-van-power-checklist/)**

## If you share the rig: the 60-second workday agreement
Say it out loud in the morning:

> “Work baseline stays. Optional comfort is conditional.  
> If we drift, we shift modes early. If we argue, we run the reset.  
> We’ll choose one recovery move and reassess later.”

## When workdays keep failing (what it usually signals)
If workdays regularly end in power stress, one of these is usually true:
- your recovery routine isn’t strong enough for your work baseline
- your workflow has silent leaks
- your plan depends on “good weather days” too much
- you’re trying to solve routine problems with last-minute decisions
