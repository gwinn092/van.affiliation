## How We Picked These Solar Options

We score every option against four budget-first criteria:

1. **Cost per usable watt** after required extras.
2. **Expandability** so your first purchase does not get replaced.
3. **Real install complexity** for first-time DIY builders.
4. **Serviceability** when parts fail on the road.

A cheap kit that is hard to expand often becomes expensive in year two.

## What to Look For in Van Solar

We’re keeping this high-level—no wiring instructions here. For installs, follow manufacturer guidance or work with a qualified installer.

- **Panel fit before watt goals:** Your roof layout controls what is realistic.
- **Controller quality:** A good MPPT controller protects battery life and harvest.
- **Wire and fuse quality:** Reliability matters more than saving $20 on copper.
- **Battery strategy:** Start small if needed, but choose components that scale.

## Common Solar Mistakes

- Buying panel wattage before measuring real roof space.
- Ignoring wire losses and fuse placement.
- Building for hypothetical future loads you may never use.
- Skipping airflow under panels in high-heat regions.

## FAQ

### How much solar do I need for van life?
For basic daily loads, 200W to 400W works for many people, depending on climate and battery size.

### Is portable solar enough full-time?
It can work for light loads, but roof-mounted solar is usually better for convenience and consistency.

### Should I start with lithium batteries?
If budget allows, lithium often wins long-term. But a phased system is better than waiting forever for a perfect system.
