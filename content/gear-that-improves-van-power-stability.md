This page is about reducing drift without rewiring your rig.
The goal is to lower baseline pressure and prevent bad-week spirals before they start.

Start with:
- **[Van Power Stability Scorecard](/van-power-stability-scorecard/)**
- **[Recovery Moves Index](/recovery-moves-index/)**

## Humidity control (protect electronics + comfort)

- Moisture absorbers (DampRid-style products) in appropriate storage areas.
- A small hygrometer so you can see humidity trends and adjust habits.
- Storage habits that keep sensitive gear dry and away from direct damp airflow.

For coastal caution and electronics-risk framing, use:
- **[Coastal Humidity Electronics Care](/coastal-humidity-electronics-care/)**

## Heat control (reduce cooling demand)

- Shade tools like windshield covers and window coverings.
- Vent and airflow habits that reduce unnecessary runtime.
- Simple insulation add-ons that reduce heat gain through exposed surfaces.

Use:
- **[Van Cooling Power Stability](/van-cooling-power-stability/)**
- **[Heat + Humidity Van Power Recovery](/heat-and-humidity-van-power-recovery/)**

## Cold control (reduce heater + device loads)

- Insulation basics and window covers to reduce overnight heat loss.
- Draft reduction habits around doors and high-leak areas.
- Sleep-first setup choices that protect baseline comfort.

Use:
- **[Cold Weather Van Power Recovery](/cold-weather-van-power-recovery/)**
- **[Cold + Cloudy Van Power Recovery](/cold-and-cloudy-van-power-recovery/)**

## Low-drift lighting + small habits

- Headlamps and low-draw task lighting for targeted use instead of broad lighting.
- Timers for non-essential routines so loads do not run longer than intended.
- Simple “off by default” habits for convenience items.

Use:
- **[Van Power Planning Mistakes](/van-power-planning-mistakes/)**

## “Recovery day” helpers

- An extension cord for legal, planned shore sessions.
- Basic power-strip discipline for intentional load control (no install guidance).
- A notebook or checklist habit to track what caused drift and what fixed it.
