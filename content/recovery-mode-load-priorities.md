Recovery mode is not punishment. It’s a **short-term stability move**.

When battery is low and stress is high, your job is to:
1) protect baseline
2) stop avoidable drain
3) recover margin on purpose

If you want the full ladder this page fits into, start here: **[Van Power Recovery Stack](/van-power-recovery-stack/)**.  
If you’re not sure whether you’re in Recovery, use: **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**.

## The Recovery Mode rule

In Recovery mode, you don’t debate every device. You decide by **category**.

That keeps this calm, fast, and fair:
- fewer arguments
- fewer accidental drains
- faster recovery

If conflict is already rising, use this first:
- **[Van Power Conflict Reset Script](/van-power-conflict-reset-script/)**

## Step 1: Protect baseline (non-negotiables)

Baseline is what keeps you safe and functional. Baseline varies by person and climate.

Examples of baseline categories:
- essential lighting and basic visibility
- required communication (the minimum you need to be safe/logistically stable)
- critical health/comfort needs (heat changes this—see below)

Heat note: if you’re in a heat wave, your baseline may include comfort loads you normally treat as optional. Use:
- **[Heat-Wave Van Power Recovery](/heat-wave-van-power-recovery/)**

**Couples rule:** baseline must be agreed on *before* you’re stressed.
- **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**
- **[Shared Van Power Check-In Template](/shared-van-power-check-in-template/)**

## Step 2: Stop leaks (the fastest “free” recovery)

Before you pause anything that affects comfort, stop the silent drains:
- tighten inverter habits
- move to DC-first where practical
- avoid leaving AC pathways running “just in case”

Use these as your leak-stop checklist:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

If you only do one thing today: **stop one leak and hold it** until you’re stable.

---

## Step 3: Pause-first categories (choose one, then reassess)

Recovery mode works best when you pause **one category at a time**, then reassess.  
Don’t stack changes. Don’t pile on restrictions. Keep it simple.

### Category A: Convenience loads
These are “nice now” but not necessary to stay stable.
- optional appliances used for convenience
- non-essential comfort extras
- “just because it’s easier” choices

**Default rule:** pause these first.

### Category B: High-draw AC workflows (especially if inverter stays on)
Not a ban—just a Recovery-mode pause until margin returns.
- AC-first workflows that have a DC alternative
- leaving AC pathways on when you’re not actively using them

Use:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

### Category C: Entertainment and background use
These are easy to underestimate because they feel small in the moment.
- background screens/speakers
- “always-on” habits
- passive consumption that runs for long stretches

**Default rule:** pause background use before you pause baseline.

### Category D: Cooking choices that trigger conflict
In Recovery mode, cooking decisions should be explicit, not emotional.
- use a decision guide instead of debating mid-hunger

Use:
- **[Cook or Conserve Power Decision Guide](/cook-or-conserve-power-decision-guide/)**

## Step 4: Pick one recovery move (don’t rely on luck)

After you’ve protected baseline and stopped leaks, choose **one** recovery path:
- shore power routine (when available)
- driving recovery (when it fits your routine)
- solar behavior aligned with conditions
