Most van power failures aren’t sudden. They’re **drift**.

A little extra comfort use here, a missed recovery window there, a few cloudy days, a late-night habit… and suddenly you’re in **Recovery mode** reacting to everything.

This weekly reset is a short routine you do once a week (or anytime the week feels “off”) to:
- stop leaks early
- choose a recovery plan **on purpose**
- reduce conflict and decision fatigue

If you want the full ladder this routine supports, start here: **[Van Power Recovery Stack](/van-power-recovery-stack/)**.

---

## What this routine is (and isn’t)

This is not a wiring guide. It’s not a teardown of your system.  
It’s a **workflow reset** built around CAVL’s recovery-first mindset:

- time-to-recover > raw storage
- stability first, upgrades later

Use the mode framework alongside this:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

## The Weekly Reset (10 minutes, no drama)

### Step 1: Name the week you’re entering
Ask:
- “Are we entering a **stable week** or a **fragile week**?”
- “Do we expect heat, clouds, heavy workdays, lots of driving, or lots of parking?”

If conditions are harsh, assume you’ll need earlier mode shifts:
- **[Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan](/cloudy-week-power-recovery-plan/)**
- **[Heat-Wave Van Power Recovery](/heat-wave-van-power-recovery/)**

---

### Step 2: Pick your default mode for the week
Don’t wait until battery stress forces the decision.

Choose a default:
- **Normal** if recovery is easy and consistent
- **Conservation** if you want to prevent drift
- **Recovery** if you’re already behind or the week is harsh

Use the system:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

---

### Step 3: Stop one leak (your highest ROI fix)
Pick **one** leak to tighten for the week. Only one.

Most common leak categories:
- inverter staying on “just in case”
- AC-first workflows when DC would work
- background entertainment and “always-on” habits
- convenience choices that quietly stack

Use:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

If you’re already in Recovery mode often, use:
- **[Recovery Mode Load Priorities](/recovery-mode-load-priorities/)**

---

### Step 4: Choose one recovery plan you can actually execute
This is the heart of the weekly reset: pick a refill path you can reliably do.

Choose one primary recovery plan:
- shore power routine (when available)
- driving recovery (when your week includes movement)
- solar behavior (when conditions and parking support it)

Use these frameworks:
- **[Shore Power Recovery Routine](/shore-power-recovery-routine/)**
- **[Solar vs Alternator Charging](/solar-vs-alternator-charging/)**
- **[Bluetti Fast Charging](/bluetti-fast-charging/)**

If the forecast/conditions are unstable, plan for earlier switches:
- **[Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan](/cloudy-week-power-recovery-plan/)**

---

### Step 5: Set two “guardrails” for the week
Guardrails are simple yes/no boundaries that prevent drift.

Examples:
- “We don’t run convenience loads until after a recovery window.”
- “We don’t leave the inverter on unless we’re actively using AC.”
- “We pause background entertainment when we’re in Conservation or Recovery.”
- “We decide cooking with the guide—no debates when hungry.”

Cooking guardrail help:
- **[Cook or Conserve Power Decision Guide](/cook-or-conserve-power-decision-guide/)**

---

### Step 6: Lock your daily anchors (morning + night)
Weekly stability comes from two tiny anchors.

- Morning: choose mode + pick a recovery window
- Night: protect baseline + set tomorrow’s first refill path

Use:
- **[Morning Van Power Check](/morning-van-power-check/)**
- **[Overnight Van Power Checklist](/overnight-van-power-checklist/)**

---

### Step 7: If you share the rig, do a 2-minute check-in
Most power conflict is unclear priorities under stress.

Do a short check-in:
- “Baseline stays.”
- “Here’s our one leak to stop.”
- “Here’s our one recovery plan.”
- “Here are the two guardrails.”

Use:
- **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**
- **[Shared Van Power Check-In Template](/shared-van-power-check-in-template/)**

If you’re already tense, start with the reset script first:
- **[Van Power Conflict Reset Script](/van-power-conflict-reset-script/)**
