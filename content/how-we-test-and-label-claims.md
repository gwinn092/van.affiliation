Every review on this site is based on one of three things:

1. **Measured** — tested firsthand with real numbers
2. **Spec** — from the manufacturer spec sheet or product page
3. **Reported** — cited from a credible third-party source (reviewer, independent test, community pattern)
4. **Estimate** — a modeled assumption based on real-world experience, clearly flagged

When a number appears on this site, it carries one of those four labels. If you see a watt-hour figure without a label, that's an error — let us know.

## Our Test Environment

- **Vehicle:** 2017 Ram ProMaster 2500, black exterior
- **Locations tested:** Arizona (high summer heat), Colorado (winter cold), Florida (summer humidity), California, New York
- **Duration:** 8 years of full-time daily use
- **Daily loads running during testing:** Dometic CFX65DZ fridge (24/7), 2x Maxxair fans (7500 and 4500), 6 LED lights, water pump, laptop, phones, iPad

The black ProMaster is a relevant detail. A black van in Arizona amplifies heat load significantly — ambient interior temperatures exceed what most people experience in other climates. Results in extreme heat are noted where they differ meaningfully from temperate conditions.

## Real Measured Data

### Dometic CFX65DZ Fridge — Power Draw (Measured)

This fridge has run 24/7 for years across all climates. Real average draw varies significantly by ambient temperature:

| Condition | Avg. Draw |
|-----------|-----------|
| Florida summer (high ambient heat) | 20–25W/hr |
| Mild climate / room temperature | 10–12W/hr |
| Cold weather | Minimal — compressor cycles rarely |

These are observed averages, not spec claims. The Florida summer number is the planning number that matters — it's what determines whether your system can sustain continuous fridge operation through a bad week.

### Bluetti Elite 200V2 — Charge Time (Measured)

Via wall outlet: approximately 1 hour to full from near-empty. (Measured, our unit — individual results may vary slightly depending on outlet quality and ambient temperature.)

### 300W Solar — Harvest Reality (Measured)

Three 100W Renogy slim panels roof-mounted on the ProMaster. Adequate on clear sunny days in good solar regions. Tight in Colorado winter or extended overcast. Would recommend 500–600W minimum if building the same system today — modern slim panels pack more output in the same footprint for similar prices.

### Goal Zero Yeti 1400 — Long-Term Durability (Measured)

Ran this unit for 6 years full-time starting 2018. Battery held up well over the cycle life — no significant capacity degradation visible in daily use. Upgraded to the Bluetti Elite 200V2 not because it failed, but because capacity needs grew and modern LFP technology offers meaningfully better recovery speed and cycle life specs.

## What We Don't Claim

We do not publish charge times we haven't observed or can't source to a credible test. When we use manufacturer input wattage specs to estimate charge time, it's labeled Spec and Estimate.

We do not combine spec numbers in ways that imply guaranteed real-world performance. A station rated at 1,200W AC input will charge faster than one rated at 400W — but real-world time-to-full depends on thermal conditions, outlet quality, and battery state, not just the spec.

We do not publish step-by-step electrical wiring procedures. Vehicle electrical systems carry real safety stakes. That's not a space for general tutorials.

## Recovery-First Framework

Create A Van Life uses a **recovery-first** lens: we rank products by how reliably they help you recover from a depleted state and return to a stable weekly routine, not by headline capacity specs alone.

We keep a **700Wh+ minimum floor** because lower-capacity units are more likely to force charge-chasing in bad weather or tight refill windows. The goal is repeatable stability, not one good day.

We prioritize **recharge speed and time-to-recover** over raw Wh because real van routines are constrained by outlet access, rules, and schedule. A unit that recovers quickly in your actual window is usually a better buy than a larger unit with slower refill behavior.
