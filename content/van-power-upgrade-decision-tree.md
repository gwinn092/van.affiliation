## Step 1 — Find the failure type (pick the first “yes”)

- If you’re losing power while “doing nothing,” then it is likely a leak problem.  
Use: **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)** and **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**.

- If you’re arguing about loads or things feel unfair, then it is likely a baseline problem.  
Use: **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)**, **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**, and **[Van Power Conflict Reset Script](/van-power-conflict-reset-script/)**.

- If you can’t reliably refill, then it is likely a recovery speed or recovery window problem.  
Use: **[Recovery Window Priority Map](/recovery-window-priority-map/)**, **[Best Fast-Charging Power Stations](/best-fast-charging-power-stations/)**, **[Shore Power Recovery Routine](/shore-power-recovery-routine/)**, and **[Solar vs Alternator Charging](/solar-vs-alternator-charging/)**.

- If your routine is solid but you still hit zero margin, then capacity may be the limiter.  
Verify with: **[Van Power Upgrade Checklist](/van-power-upgrade-checklist/)**. Then use: **[Van Power Upgrade Paths](/van-power-upgrade-paths/)** and **[What 700Wh Actually Runs](/what-700wh-actually-runs/)**.

## Step 2 — Pick today’s move (one move only)

- Stop one leak for a week.
- Protect baseline with a comfort agreement.
- Choose one recovery window each day.
- Plan one refill move based on shore, drive, or solar reality.
