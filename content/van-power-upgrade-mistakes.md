Upgrades should be deliberate, not panic buttons. The pattern that usually works is simple:

**Fix leaks → protect baseline → improve recovery speed → then add capacity.**

- **[Van Power Upgrade Checklist](/van-power-upgrade-checklist/)**

## Mistake 1 — Buying more Wh instead of improving recovery speed

Pattern:
- Buying bigger storage first while refill windows stay weak or inconsistent.

Why it backfires:
- A larger battery can still feel unstable if recovery stays slow.

Do this instead:
- Choose the strongest refill move you can execute in real life, then size capacity around that routine.

Use:
- **[Recovery Window Priority Map](/recovery-window-priority-map/)**
- **[Best Fast-Charging Power Stations](/best-fast-charging-power-stations/)**

## Mistake 2 — Treating a bad week like a permanent needs problem

Pattern:
- One harsh week triggers a panic purchase.

Why it backfires:
- Temporary conditions can distort what your normal system actually needs.

Do this instead:
- Run a bad-week protocol first, then decide if the issue repeats across normal weeks.

## Mistake 3 — Upgrading gear while routines leak

Pattern:
- New gear is added while old workflow losses stay untouched.

Why it backfires:
- Passive drain and AC-first habits keep burning margin.

Do this instead:
- Tighten leak points before deciding your next hardware move.

- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**

## Mistake 4 — Building the plan around “solar will handle it”

Pattern:
- Planning around ideal harvest every day.

Why it backfires:
- Shade, clouds, and seasonal angle can collapse expected recovery.

Do this instead:
- Treat solar as one layer, not the only layer.

- **[Solar vs Alternator Charging](/solar-vs-alternator-charging/)**

## Mistake 5 — Not setting a comfort baseline (especially couples)

Pattern:
- People assume the same priorities without defining them.

Why it backfires:
- Under stress, power decisions turn into fairness arguments.

Do this instead:
- Define baseline vs optional comfort and use scripts when tension rises.

Pattern:
- Treating tier pages like fixed price guarantees.

Why it backfires:
- Real-world pricing moves, and “always under” assumptions break quickly.

- Use an “under now” vs “more picks” mindset and check current status before buying.
