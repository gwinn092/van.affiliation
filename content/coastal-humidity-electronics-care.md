Coastal humidity is different. Moisture plus salt air can be harder on electronics than inland humidity alone.

In our experience, risk can increase in very humid and salty conditions. Always follow manufacturer care guidance for your specific equipment.

Start here:
- **[Humidity Night Power Stability](/humidity-night-power-stability/)**

## The pattern we’ve seen (cautious field note)

In very humid and salty air, long overnight roof-fan runtime may increase moisture and salt exposure risk for electronics, including some control boards.

This is not universal. Outcomes vary by local conditions, runtime habits, and equipment design.

## Practical, low-risk steps (non-technical)

- Reduce direct salty airflow on sensitive gear when possible through placement and basic shielding.
- Use moisture absorbers (for example, DampRid) where appropriate.
- Air-dry the rig on lower-humidity days when you can.
- Wipe down exposed surfaces and maintain vents/filters per manufacturer guidance.
- If something starts acting up, stop use and contact manufacturer support.
