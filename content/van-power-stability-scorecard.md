## How to use this scorecard (30 seconds)

Mark each section:
- ✅ stable
- ⚠️ shaky
- ❌ failing

Then use the **What to do next** section to pick your next move.

## Section 1 — Leaks (hidden drain)

- If you lose meaningful battery while “doing nothing,” your workflow likely has leak points.
- If inverter use stays on by default, you’re probably burning margin passively.
- If AC convenience is the default path for small tasks, drift usually compounds.

Use:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**

## Section 2 — Baseline (what stays on)

- Can you name your baseline loads in one sentence?
- If you share the rig, do you have a comfort-budget agreement?
- Do both people know what stays on when things get tight?

Use:
- **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)**
- **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**

## Section 3 — Recovery windows (refill reality)

- Do you have at least one reliable daily recovery window?
- Do quiet hours, parking constraints, or clouds regularly break your plan?
- Can you say what your next refill move is before stress hits?

Use:
- **[Recovery Window Priority Map](/recovery-window-priority-map/)**
- **[Quiet Hours Recovery Plan](/quiet-hours-recovery-plan/)**
- **[Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan](/cloudy-week-power-recovery-plan/)**

## Section 4 — Bad-week readiness (mode switch early)

- Do you switch into Conservation or Recovery before the spiral?
- Do you tighten behavior early when conditions are getting harsh?
- Can you run a bad-week routine without debating every decision?

Use:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**
- **[Tier 2 Bad Week Protocol](/tier-2-bad-week-protocol/)**

  Use: **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)** and **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**.
