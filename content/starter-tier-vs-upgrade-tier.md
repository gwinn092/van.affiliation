Budget questions are really stability questions.

The under-$500 tier can absolutely work—**if your routine supports it**.  
The under-$1000 tier is usually about one thing: **faster recovery windows** so you don’t spiral.

This page helps you choose a tier without hype or regret.

## The CAVL rule that decides everything
We prioritize **time-to-recover** over raw storage.

If your life repeatedly creates “behind” days (heat, humidity, clouds, work pressure, quiet-hours constraints), you don’t just need capacity—you need a recovery window you can execute.

## When the Under-$500 starter tier is enough
Starter tier is often enough when most of these are true:

- Your baseline needs are modest and consistent
- You can execute recovery reliably (even if it’s not fast)
- You don’t have frequent multi-day “bad conditions” stretches
- You use strong workflow discipline (DC-first, inverter habits)
- You’re willing to run Conservation earlier when needed

## When you should move up to the Under-$1000 upgrade tier
Upgrade tier is usually worth it when any of these are true:

- You’re often in **Recovery mode** (or flirting with it)
- You have repeated “night drift” (heat/humidity/quiet hours)
- You work from the van and can’t afford instability
- You get stuck in cloudy stretches where recovery windows disappear
- You can’t rely on movement/drive recovery often enough
- You share the rig and conflict spikes when power is tight
