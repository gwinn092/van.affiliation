This page is for people using an **under-$500** style setup (CALV floor still applies: **700Wh+**) who want stability without constantly “needing more.”

## The starter-tier rule: your system must be recoverable
On Tier 1, you don’t have slack.

So you win by:
- choosing mode early
- stopping leaks aggressively
- protecting a real recovery window
- preventing night drift before it starts

Mode framework:
- **[Daily Van Power Mode System](/daily-van-power-mode-system/)**

---

## Step 1: Define your baseline (what stays)
Baseline is what you protect even when you’re tight.

Define baseline as categories:
- sleep protection needs (your reality)
- minimum lighting for safety
- minimum communication/logistics stability
- work baseline if you’re remote

If you share the rig, agree on baseline:
- **[Van Power Comfort Budget](/van-power-comfort-budget/)**  
- **[Shared Van Power Rules for Couples](/shared-van-power-rules-for-couples/)**

## Step 2: Stop leaks first (Tier 1 lives or dies here)
Most Tier 1 failures are leaks, not “not enough battery.”

Two big leak sources:
- inverter habits
- AC-first workflows when DC would work

Use:
- **[Inverter On or Off Workflow](/inverter-on-or-off-workflow/)**  
- **[DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows](/dc-vs-ac-in-van-life-workflows/)**  

Tier 1 rule:
> If it doesn’t protect baseline, it doesn’t run “by default.”
