Nine times out of ten it's one of these three — in this order:

## How we think about power here

Most van life power advice is written for perfect conditions: sunny days, campground hookups, no schedule pressure. That's not most van life.

Real van life has cloudy stretches, campground quiet hours, heavy work weeks, and parking constraints you didn't plan for. The question that actually matters isn't *"what works when things go well?"* — it's **"what keeps me stable through my worst week?"**

That shifts what matters:
- **Recharge speed often beats raw capacity** when your recovery windows are short
- **Alternator charging while you drive** is consistently underrated against solar
- **One failure on a bad week** matters more than impressive specs on a good one

Every specific number on this site is labeled so you know what you're actually reading:
- **Spec** — the manufacturer's published figure
- **Reported** — from a third-party test or review
- **Measured** — a controlled test with a cited source
- **Estimate** — reasoned math, not a hard claim

Van life gear marketing blurs these constantly. Here, you always know which one you're looking at.
