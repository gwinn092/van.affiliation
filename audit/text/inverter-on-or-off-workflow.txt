A Simple Workflow That Saves Power # Most “mystery drain” is workflow drain. In many van setups, the problem is not one big mistake, it is small AC habits stacking all day and night. Default OFF is a strategy. A better inverter routine usually improves stability faster than people expect. What an inverter does (in plain English) # Your battery stores DC energy. An inverter converts that DC into AC so you can use household-style plugs. That conversion is useful, but it adds overhead. This is why AC convenience can feel expensive during weak recovery windows. When leaving it ON makes sense # Leaving the inverter on can make sense when: You are actively using AC for a defined task window You have predictable AC blocks in your day You are in a controlled recovery context and know your next refill window The key is intentional use, not default use. When leaving it ON hurts you # Leaving inverter mode on can hurt stability when: You leave it on overnight without needing AC You are in a cloudy-week recovery squeeze Your system is already tight and refill windows are uncertain Background convenience loads creep into your routine In those conditions, small workflow losses compound quickly. The CAVL inverter workflow (simple routine) # Rule 1: Default OFF. Turn AC on only when you’re actively using it. Rule 2: Use AC in planned windows. Do the task, then return to DC-first. Rule 3: Protect overnight margin. Baseline loads first (fridge, ventilation, essential devices). Rule 4: Tighten earlier in weak weather. Don’t wait until you’re already behind. Rule 5: Shore day resets—then discipline returns. Recover intentionally, then go back to routine.
