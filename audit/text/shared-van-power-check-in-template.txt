A short shared check-in protects baseline stability and keeps small decisions from turning into daily stress. Mantra: pick a mode, protect baseline, plan comfort on purpose. How to use this template # Keep this page open in Notes, screenshots, or a pinned browser tab. Copy/paste the scripts into your daily check-in note. Run it once in the morning and once before bed. Keep answers short and practical. If you disagree, choose the tighter mode for now and re-check later. Step 1: Pick today’s mode # Choose one mode before making comfort decisions: Normal: recovery is reliable and baseline is stable. Conservation: recovery is uncertain; tighten optional usage. Recovery: you are behind; reset first, comfort second. Choose the tighter mode for now, then re-check after the next recovery window. What if we forget to do it? # Attach the script to existing habits: first coffee and lights-out routine. Do we need to do this forever? # Not always at full detail. Most people use it daily during variable weather, then simplify when routines are stable. What if one person needs more comfort than the other? # Use planned comfort windows instead of all-day defaults. This protects both comfort and stability. Can roommates or families use this too? # Yes. The template works for any shared power setup. What is the point of the watch-out line? # It surfaces risk early (heat, clouds, schedule shifts) before small decisions compound.
