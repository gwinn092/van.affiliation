The Comfort Budget idea (simple on purpose) # A comfort budget is not a spreadsheet. It’s an agreement. You decide in calm conditions what your rig protects when conditions get hard. This works best when you already use: Daily Van Power Mode System Van Power Weekly Reset Routine Step 1: Define your “Baseline” (protected category) # Baseline = what keeps you safe, functional, and stable. Baseline is personal and climate-dependent. In heat, baseline often expands. Heat note: Heat-Wave Van Power Recovery Write your baseline as categories , not devices: safety/visibility minimum communication for logistics/safety sleep protection needs (varies by climate/season) food safety routines (your reality, not someone else’s) Couples rule: baseline must be agreed on together. Examples of optional comfort categories: convenience appliances and “nice to have” routines background entertainment extended “AC-first” workflows comfort upgrades that are great when you can recover The condition is simple: Optional comfort happens after you’ve protected recovery. One to three categories is enough. Step 4: Tie comfort to mode (so you don’t renegotiate daily) # This is the whole point: reduce negotiations.
