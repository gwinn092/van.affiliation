A 700Wh label tells you battery capacity, not guaranteed device runtime. What matters in practice is usable energy , not just rated capacity. Real use is reduced by: Inverter and conversion losses AC vs DC delivery path differences Battery reserve behavior and operating limits Cycling loads that turn on and off through the day That is why two people with “700Wh” can report different results. Reality rule: AC use often costs more than people expect. DC-first routines usually stretch a small system further. The reality factor: what changes your runtime # Heat and humidity: fridge duty cycle rises and fans run longer. AC vs DC usage: AC-heavy routines can lose more energy through conversion. Cooking spikes: short high-draw sessions can remove margin quickly. Cloudy days: lower harvest increases pressure on recovery plans. FAQ # Can 700Wh run a fridge overnight? # Sometimes, depending on fridge behavior, ambient temperature, and what else is running. Treat it as possible, not guaranteed. Why does my “700Wh” feel smaller than expected? # Usable energy is lower than label capacity once conversion losses, cycling loads, and reserve behavior are included. Is 700Wh enough for full-time? # It can work for lighter routines with disciplined use and reliable recovery. Many full-time setups eventually prefer more buffer. What about cooking? # Occasional spikes can be manageable. Daily high-draw cooking changes system requirements and usually pushes toward larger buffer and stronger recovery. Should I buy bigger capacity or faster charging? # For many van users, faster recovery improves day-to-day stability sooner than a moderate capacity increase alone. Can solar alone carry my setup? # Sometimes in ideal exposure patterns. Mixed weather and shade usually require backup recovery planning. Is alternator charging worth adding? # It can be a strong driving-recovery layer when your route pattern supports it and installation is done safely. What is the biggest planning mistake? # Sizing for ideal days and ignoring poor-weather recovery.
