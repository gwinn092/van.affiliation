Start here (recovery-first): Solar vs Alternator Charging BLUETTI Fast Charging for Van Life What 700Wh Actually Runs The cloudy-week failure pattern: the slow spiral # The slow spiral is simple: one weak recovery day becomes two, then three. Fridge and fan loads continue, harvest drops, and margin disappears. Heat and humidity can make this worse by pushing comfort loads higher. In winter or low-sun periods, weak harvest compounds the same pattern. Cloudy-week rule: don’t spend tomorrow’s power tonight. CAVL Field Notes (real-world experience) # Cloudy stretches feel heavier in heat because comfort pressure stays high while recovery windows get weaker. In humid climates, drift often shows up overnight, then compounds by midday if no refill move is chosen. Picking one recovery move early is usually calmer than trying to fix everything late. Step 1: Separate baseline loads from comfort loads # Start by splitting your routine into two buckets: Baseline loads: fridge, essential ventilation, essential device charging Comfort loads: optional extras, convenience loads, non-essential usage Quick examples: Baseline: fridge, one fan overnight if needed, phone/laptop essentials Comfort: extra fans, entertainment, convenience cooking, “nice-to-have” charging This one step reduces panic decisions. Baseline loads stay protected, comfort loads become flexible when recovery is weak. Step 2: Build your 3-layer recovery plan (shore + driving + solar) # Shore: your stability anchor when available Driving: your recovery layer when travel pattern supports it Solar: your harvest layer when conditions cooperate Cloudy-week stability usually comes from layering all three where possible, not depending on one source. Read next: Solar vs Alternator Charging BLUETTI Fast Charging for Van Life Step 3: The cloudy-week routine (daily workflow) # Morning # Check your current margin honestly. Prioritize refill windows first. Avoid surprise AC-heavy usage early. Midday # Harvest if possible. Reposition for exposure if practical. Preserve baseline loads; delay optional loads. Evening # Protect margin for overnight essentials. Make a next-day recovery plan before bed. Avoid “just this once” high-draw decisions when already behind. The ‘cloudy week’ power menu (what to do when you’re behind) # If you can plug in today: reset now. Anchor recovery immediately, then rebuild tomorrow around baseline loads. If you’ll drive today: use it as recovery. Treat driving as a refill window; keep optional loads low until margin is rebuilt. If you can’t do either: protect baseline. Trim to essentials, preserve overnight stability, and schedule the next refill window as your top priority. Where 700Wh fits (and when it’s not enough) # At CAVL, 700Wh is the minimum recommendation baseline because lower tiers often become fragile under normal van routines. In cloudy periods, 700Wh can still work with disciplined usage and strong recovery habits. Larger classes usually feel calmer because they provide more buffer while you wait for the next good refill window.
