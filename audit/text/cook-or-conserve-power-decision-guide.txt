A Van Life Power Decision Guide for Electric Cooking Days # Electric cooking is not just a load decision. It is a recovery decision. The question is not only “can I cook now?” The real question is “what does this cost my stability tomorrow?” Mantra: cook on recovery days, conserve on uncertain days. The rule: cook on recovery days, conserve on uncertain days # If your refill window is reliable, cooking can fit. If recovery is uncertain, conserving usually protects your next day. This single rule reduces most cooking-day regret. Step 1: What mode are you in today? # Before deciding to cook, identify your day mode: Normal Conservation Recovery Use this guide first: Daily Van Power Mode System Step 2: Use the 3-question test # Q1: Do I have a reliable recovery window today or tomorrow? # If yes: cooking may fit if you stay planned. If no: conserve first and protect baseline. Q2: Are baseline loads already heavy (heat, fans, fridge)? # If yes: cooking demand stacks faster than expected. If no: you have more flexibility, but still plan recovery. Q3: Will cooking stack on top of other AC-heavy tasks today? # If yes: separate tasks into planned windows instead of stacking. If no: cooking can be easier to absorb. Decision rule: if two or more answers point to risk, conserve today . Step 3: If you cook, do it intentionally # Choose a planned cooking window. Avoid stacking with other optional AC-heavy tasks. Keep your next recovery window visible before you start. Return to baseline-first behavior after cooking. Choose no-power / low-power meals on uncertain days. Shift optional tasks to a better recovery day. Plan your next shore/driving/solar recovery window now. Small systems usually need stricter timing and stronger discipline. Is cooking on AC always worse? # Not always. The issue is unplanned stacking and poor recovery timing, not AC by itself. Should I cook while charging on shore power? # That can be a practical window, but keep it intentional and avoid surprise stacking that hides tomorrow’s risk. What’s the best way to avoid regret tomorrow? # Decide mode first, run the 3-question test, and lock recovery windows before cooking. Is conservation mode just “no cooking ever”? # No. It means cooking decisions are stricter until stability returns. Does this only matter in bad weather? # Bad weather makes it obvious, but cooking-day planning matters in all conditions. What if my partner wants electric cooking daily? # Use a shared mode rule and planned cooking windows so comfort goals and stability goals can coexist. How does this tie into bigger systems? # Bigger systems add buffer, but routine still decides whether your week feels stable.
