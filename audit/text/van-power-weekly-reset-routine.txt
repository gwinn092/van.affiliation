This weekly reset is a short routine you do once a week (or anytime the week feels “off”) to: stop leaks early choose a recovery plan on purpose reduce conflict and decision fatigue If you want the full ladder this routine supports, start here: Van Power Recovery Stack . What this routine is (and isn’t) # This is not a wiring guide. It’s not a teardown of your system. It’s a workflow reset built around CAVL’s recovery-first mindset: time-to-recover > raw storage stability first, upgrades later Use the mode framework alongside this: Daily Van Power Mode System The Weekly Reset (10 minutes, no drama) # Step 1: Name the week you’re entering # Ask: “Are we entering a stable week or a fragile week ?” “Do we expect heat, clouds, heavy workdays, lots of driving, or lots of parking?” If conditions are harsh, assume you’ll need earlier mode shifts: Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan Heat-Wave Van Power Recovery Step 2: Pick your default mode for the week # Don’t wait until battery stress forces the decision. Choose a default: Normal if recovery is easy and consistent Conservation if you want to prevent drift Recovery if you’re already behind or the week is harsh Use the system: Daily Van Power Mode System Step 3: Stop one leak (your highest ROI fix) # Pick one leak to tighten for the week. Only one. Most common leak categories: inverter staying on “just in case” AC-first workflows when DC would work background entertainment and “always-on” habits convenience choices that quietly stack Use: Inverter On or Off Workflow DC vs AC in Van Life Workflows If you’re already in Recovery mode often, use: Recovery Mode Load Priorities Step 4: Choose one recovery plan you can actually execute # This is the heart of the weekly reset: pick a refill path you can reliably do. Choose one primary recovery plan: shore power routine (when available) driving recovery (when your week includes movement) solar behavior (when conditions and parking support it) Use these frameworks: Shore Power Recovery Routine Solar vs Alternator Charging Bluetti Fast Charging If the forecast/conditions are unstable, plan for earlier switches: Cloudy Week Power Recovery Plan Step 5: Set two “guardrails” for the week # Guardrails are simple yes/no boundaries that prevent drift. Examples: “We don’t run convenience loads until after a recovery window.” “We don’t leave the inverter on unless we’re actively using AC.” “We pause background entertainment when we’re in Conservation or Recovery.” “We decide cooking with the guide—no debates when hungry.” Cooking guardrail help: Cook or Conserve Power Decision Guide Step 6: Lock your daily anchors (morning + night) # Weekly stability comes from two tiny anchors.
