Van Power Conflict Reset Script (30–60 Seconds) 22 February 2026 · 5 mins Table of Contents Table of Contents When the battery is low, everything feels personal . Heat, devices, hunger, bad sleep, noise—your nervous system treats it like an emergency. This page gives you a short reset script you can use in the moment to stop the spiral and switch into Recovery mode thinking . If you haven’t set shared rules yet, start here: Shared Van Power Rules for Couples . If you want a repeatable structure, keep this open: Shared Van Power Check-In Template . What this script does (and what it doesn’t) # This script is not about solving the whole trip, picking a new power station, or “winning” the argument. It is a fast pivot from blame → plan , using the same framework as the Daily Van Power Mode System : Normal when you can coast Conservation when you need to slow burn Recovery when you must restore margin When battery is low and stress is high, assume you’re in Recovery until proven otherwise. The 30–60 second reset script (say it out loud) # Use the exact words below or adapt them to your voice. The point is tone and sequence , not perfection. Version A: Simple and direct # Name the moment “We’re both stressed, and the battery is low. This is a power problem, not a relationship problem.” Agree on the goal “Our goal is to get stable again—not to argue about how we got here.” Choose the mode “Let’s switch to Recovery mode for now.” Pick one next action “Next step: we do the quick check , then decide one recovery move.” Set a short timeout “Can we pause the debate until after the check-in?” Then do the check: Morning framework: Morning Van Power Check Night framework: Overnight Van Power Checklist Version B: Softer / more emotional safety # “I’m feeling the pressure and I think you are too. I don’t want power stress to turn into a fight. Can we take one minute, switch to Recovery mode , and do the quick check together?” Then use the Shared Van Power Check-In Template . Version C: One-person starter (when only one of you is regulated) # “I’m getting reactive. I’m going to reset us: Recovery mode , quick check, one next step. I’m on your team.” Then initiate the check and offer two choices: “Do you want to run the checklist, or should I?” What to do immediately after the script # You only need one recovery move.
